# Bangladesh — Birth Registration for Refugee and Stateless Children

> Simplified caseworker reference for children born to Rohingya and other refugee/stateless parents in Bangladesh, with primary focus on the Cox's Bazar camps (Kutupalong, Balukhali, Nayapara). This is operational guidance for triage, not legal advice.

## Legal and policy basis

- Births and Deaths Registration Act, 2004 governs civil birth registration in Bangladesh and is administered through the Office of the Registrar General, Birth and Death Registration (under the Local Government Division).
- Rohingya refugees are managed under the Office of the Refugee Relief and Repatriation Commissioner (RRRC), which operates under the Ministry of Disaster Management and Relief.
- RRRC Circular 2018 — Proof of Birth for camp-born children establishes that births of children born in the camps are recorded through a Proof of Birth / birth notification process jointly administered by the Camp-in-Charge (CiC), RRRC, and UNHCR.
- UNHCR biometric registration (the joint Government of Bangladesh–UNHCR verification exercise) provides the underlying identity record for camp residents via the Family Registration Card (FRC) / smart identity card.

## Important distinction

A Proof of Birth document issued in the camp records the fact and circumstances of the birth. It is not the same as a Bangladeshi national birth certificate and does not by itself confer Bangladeshi nationality. For Rohingya children it serves as the primary civil-documentation record and is essential for protection, family reunification, and any future status determination.

## Who this pathway applies to

- Children born inside the Cox's Bazar camps to registered refugee parents.
- Children of stateless Rohingya parents who hold UNHCR / RRRC documentation.
- Late registration cases where the birth was not recorded near the time of delivery.

## Step-by-step registration pathway

1. Obtain a birth notification slip from the camp health facility or the attending health partner (e.g., the SARPV / partner clinic) where the delivery occurred. If the birth happened at home, proceed to step 2 for a substitute.
2. Report the birth to the Majhi (block leader) and obtain confirmation; the block leader's statement establishes the child's presence and parentage within the block.
3. Visit the Camp-in-Charge (CiC) office with the parents' UNHCR Family Registration Card (FRC) / smart card and the birth notification or block-leader confirmation.
4. The CiC office, with RRRC and UNHCR, records the child in the family record and issues / updates the Proof of Birth and the family's registration record.
5. For late registration, submit a written explanation for the delay together with the available secondary evidence; the RRRC reviews and records the birth retroactively.

## Required documents

- Parents' UNHCR Family Registration Card (FRC) or smart identity card.
- Birth notification from the camp health facility (date, place, and attendant of birth).
- Block-leader (Majhi) confirmation of the family and the child.
- Mother's identity record from the biometric registration.

## Accepted substitutes when originals are unavailable

- Birth health record → witness statement from the Majhi (block leader), countersigned by the camp leader, attesting to the birth.
- Parent passport or national ID (Rohingya families rarely hold these) → UNHCR Family Registration Card is the accepted primary identity substitute.
- Father's documentation, where the father is absent or deceased → the mother's FRC plus a block-leader statement and, where available, a UNHCR protection record may substitute; absent-father cases should be flagged.

## Submission office

Camp-in-Charge (CiC) office of the relevant camp, working with the RRRC Proof of Birth process, Cox's Bazar.

## Estimated timeline

Typically 2–4 weeks for camp-born children with complete documentation; longer for late registration or cases requiring additional verification.

## Special notes and common blockers

- Absent or missing father: the father's presence may be expected for the family record. Where the father is missing, draft a contingency explanation and flag for caseworker supervisor review.
- No health facility record (home births) are common and are routinely resolved with block-leader witness statements.
- A Proof of Birth does not grant nationality; manage family expectations accordingly.
